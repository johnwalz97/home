The Pitch

Why Portland punches above its weight

A compact, walkable peninsula where a working harbor, cobblestone streets, world-class restaurants, and a string of iconic lighthouses all sit within a few minutes of each other.

🦞Eat like a champion

A perennial James Beard force — Maine sent 10 semifinalists to the 2025 awards. Lobster rolls, oysters, and duck-fat fries are just the start.

🍺Drink local

Roughly 20+ breweries in the city alone, on two walkable crawls. Allagash White and Bissell's "The Substance" are institutions.

🗺️See it on foot

Old Port, the Arts District, the West End, and the Eastern Promenade are all within a 20-minute walk. A car is only for day trips.

Your Home Base

The Old Port & the neighborhoods

⚓ Old Port

Historic cobblestone waterfront district of 19th-century brick — boutiques, galleries, seafood, breweries, nightlife, and the ferry terminal. The heart of any visit.

Free to wander · most hotels & the Casco Bay ferry are a few blocks away

🎭 Arts District / Congress St

Portland's cultural spine — the Portland Museum of Art, Maine College of Art, theaters, and the symphony.

Walkable from Old Port

🌅 East End / Munjoy Hill

Artsy neighborhood wrapped around the Eastern Promenade, with the Observatory and East End Beach. Best sunrises in the city.

~20-min walk from Old Port

🏛️ West End

One of the best-preserved Victorian neighborhoods in the country — brick mansions, gardens, Victoria Mansion, and White Mountain views from the Western Prom.

A scenic stroll rather than a shopping hub
Landmarks & Museums

History you can walk into

🖼️ Portland Museum of Art

Maine's largest art museum (1882) with a notable Winslow Homer & Andrew Wyeth collection.

$20 adult · 21 & under free · free Fri eves 4–8pm

🔭 Portland Observatory

An 1807 wooden maritime signal tower on Munjoy Hill — the last of its kind in the U.S. Climb it for bay views.

~$10 · seasonal (late May–mid-Oct) · sunset tours in summer

🏰 Victoria Mansion

An opulent 1858–60 Italianate villa with ~90% of its original interiors intact — frescoes, stained glass, carved wood.

May 1–Oct 31 · ~$20 · reserve ahead, weekends sell out

✒️ Wadsworth-Longfellow House

Portland's oldest peninsula building (1785) and the poet's boyhood home, with original furnishings and a quiet garden.

Seasonal guided tours · garden free (May–Oct)

🕊️ Portland Freedom Trail

A free, self-guided ~2-mile walk to 13 sites of African American & Underground Railroad history, incl. the Abyssinian Meeting House.

Free anytime · grab the map from mainehistory.org

🛍️ Exchange Street

The main shopping street for Maine-made goods — Venn + Maker, Maine Craft Portland, plus the Saturday Market for handmade crafts.

Time it for a Saturday market
The Beacons

Lighthouses

Four classic lights within ~20 minutes of downtown — a half-day driving loop on its own.

💡 Portland Head Light

Maine's oldest (lit 1791), on dramatic cliffs in 90-acre Fort Williams Park. The most-photographed lighthouse in the state.

~10 min · grounds free · go early for light & parking

🐞 Bug Light

A tiny, ornate 1875 cast-iron light modeled on a Greek monument, with skyline views and big kite-flying lawns.

Free park · great for sunset over the skyline

🔌 Spring Point Ledge Light

A "spark-plug" light at the end of a 900-ft granite breakwater you can actually walk out on.

Breakwater free · tours ~$10 (Jul–Oct) · mind the tide

🌊 Two Lights State Park

Rocky open-ocean ledges in Cape Elizabeth — and the Lobster Shack restaurant right on the point.

~15 min · small day-use fee · bring exact change
On the Water

Islands, ferries & sea kayaks

⛴️ Casco Bay Lines

The nation's oldest continuously operating ferry, serving the "Calendar Islands" year-round. The narrated Mailboat Run (~2.5–3.5 hrs) is the cheapest way out on the bay.

From 56 Commercial St · ~$14 round-trip in peak season

🚲 Peaks Island

The closest island — a ~17-min ferry, then rent a bike and ride the flat ~4-mile perimeter loop past beaches and old forts.

No car needed · family-friendly · lunch at Island Lobster Co.

🛶 Portland Paddle

Guided sea-kayak tours from East End Beach — sunset paddles and trips out to Civil War-era Fort Gorges.

Beginners welcome · reserve in summer

🐋 Cruises & whale watching

Narrated lighthouse cruises (Portland Discovery) and offshore whale watches (Odyssey, New England EcoAdventures).

Book ahead in summer · bring layers, it's cold offshore
Fresh Air

In-town outdoors & beaches

🌳 Eastern Promenade & Back Cove

An Olmsted-designed waterfront park (1905) with a paved 2.1-mi shoreline trail that links to the ~3.5-mi Back Cove loop — together a 5–6 mile waterfront circuit.

Free, year-round · East End Beach for a city swim

🏖️ Nearby beaches

Crescent Beach (~15 min, closest sand), Higgins & Scarborough (~20 min, surf), and famous Old Orchard Beach (~25 min, 7 miles + amusement park).

Arrive before mid-morning on hot days — parking fills fast
  • Best free experience in the city: sunrise over Casco Bay from the Eastern Prom.
  • Kayak & SUP launches, a boat ramp, and a playground all line the waterfront.
Eat · Part One

Lobster, oysters & the big tables

🦞 Eventide Oyster Co.

The town's most famous bite — a brown-butter lobster roll on a steamed bao bun, plus a pristine raw bar. James Beard-honored.

No reservations — go at opening to beat the line

🌭 Highroller Lobster Co.

Playful, casual Connecticut-style warm-butter rolls — plus lobster corn dogs and "lobster lollipops."

Order at the counter · 104 Exchange St

🔥 Fore Street

The restaurant that put Portland on the map (1996) — wood-fired, hyper-local New England cooking. Maine's #1 on OpenTable 2025.

Hardest table in town — book well ahead

🦪 Scales

Upscale seafood on a working wharf — oysters, champagne, the day's local catch. OpenTable Top 100 in America.

Reserve for a waterfront table

🍣 Mr. Tuna

Sustainable sushi & seafood; 2025 James Beard semifinalist and a Food & Wine Best New Chef.

Grew from a food cart — now a destination

🍤 Portland Lobster Company

The quintessential casual waterfront roll, with live music on an outdoor deck on Commercial St.

Peak-summer classic
Eat · Part Two

Fries, donuts & morning buns

🍟 Duckfat

Belgian fries fried in duck fat, served in a cone with five sauces, plus poutine and milkshakes. Beard-winning chef.

Small space — the Friteshack window is the quick line

🍩 The Holy Donut

Maine potato-based donuts — ~20 rotating flavors; chocolate-sea-salt is the bestseller. GF-friendly options.

Top flavors sell out — go in the morning

🥐 Standard Baking Co.

Portland's benchmark European bakery since 1995 — baguettes, almond croissants, and the legendary toffee-topped morning bun.

Below Fore Street · best items go early

🏆 ZU & Norimoto

Recent James Beard bakery winners — ZU took Outstanding Bakery; Norimoto's pastry chef won Outstanding Baker.

Plus Tandem Coffee & Bakery for locals

🥬 Portland Farmers' Market

Deering Oaks Park, Sat & Wed 7am–1pm (mid-Apr–Nov), with an indoor winter market Dec–Apr.

Go early Saturday for produce & Maine cheese

🦪 The $1 oyster scene

Same-day local oysters everywhere — Jacqueline leads the happy-hour pack with house hot-sauce droppers.

It gets packed — go right at happy hour
Drink · Beer

America's most brewery-dense city

20+ breweries in town, clustered into two walkable crawls — do each as one outing.

Crawl 1 Industrial Way

"Brewery Row." 1 Industrial Way alone holds Foundation (hazy IPAs + Detroit pizza), Austin Street, and Battery Steele ("Flume"). Allagash (the iconic White) and Definitive are a short walk away.

Book Allagash's free tour ahead · Foundation closed Tue

Crawl 2 East Bayside

Closer to downtown — Rising Tide, Goodfire, and Lone Pine (Goodfire is tucked right behind it), plus distilleries in the same blocks.

Walkable from the Old Port
  • Don't-miss pours: Allagash White · Bissell Brothers "The Substance" (at scenic Thompson's Point) · Maine Beer Co.'s "Lunch" (in Freeport).
Drink · Spirits

Cocktails & distilleries

🍸 Portland Hunt & Alpine Club

Nationally recognized, Scandinavian-inspired cocktails — a Portland benchmark since 2013.

75 Market St, Old Port

🚢 Blyth & Burrows

A multi-level, maritime-themed bar with hidden speakeasy spaces — named a best cocktail bar by Wine Enthusiast.

Old Port

🥃 New England Distilling

Portland's first distillery since Prohibition — small-batch rye, rum, and gin, with free tours & tastings.

113 Franklin St

🍹 Room for Improvement

A buzzy new Old Port bar named to Punch magazine's five best new bars in America.

Old Port

🌿 Hardshore Distilling

East End gin specialist — rosemary, mint, and Tuscan juniper, milled on site.

53 Washington Ave

🍶 Three of Strong

Small-batch rum made with Sebago Lake water; tours by advance sign-up.

35B Diamond St · reserve ahead
Beyond the City

Day trips

🥾 Freeport & L.L. Bean

The flagship store has no door locks — open 24/7/365 since 1951, with a 3,500-gallon trout tank and a giant Bean Boot out front.

~20 min north · pair with Maine Beer Co.

🎡 Old Orchard Beach

7 miles of sand, a classic boardwalk, and New England's only beachfront amusement park (Palace Playland).

~25 min · peaks Jul–Aug

⛵ Kennebunkport

A classic, well-heeled coastal village — walkable center, harbor strolls, and shopping.

~35 min south · best late spring–fall

🏞️ Cape Elizabeth

The easiest half-day — Portland Head Light, Two Lights, and Kettle Cove all in one upscale coastal town.

~10–15 min · great on a clear day

💡 Lighthouse Trail

String together Portland Head, Two Lights, Spring Point & Bug Light into a self-guided loop — several within 20 min.

Half- to full-day · best in summer/fall

🏔️ Acadia National Park

Maine's only national park — granite peaks, carriage roads, Cadillac Mountain sunrises.

~3.5 hrs each way — make it an overnight, not a day trip
When To Go

Reading the seasons

☀️ Summer (Jun–Aug)

The headline season — full ferry & tour schedules, beaches, festivals (the Old Port Festival), and temps that rarely top 80°F.

High season — book lodging early, expect crowds

🍂 Fall (Sep–Oct)

Crisp air, foliage peaking early-to-mid October, and the summer crowds gone. Harvest on the Harbor showcases the food scene.

The sweet spot for value + color

❄️ Winter

Snowy, quiet, and cheapest — with the February Winter Light Festival. Many seasonal sites are closed.

Best for budget travel & a calm city

Most museums, lighthouses' interiors, and historic houses run roughly May/June–October. Confirm 2026 hours and prices on official sites before you go.

Sun AM
Drive to Cape Elizabeth for Portland Head Light & Fort Williams. Fries at the Lobster Shack at Two Lights.
Sun PM
Detour to Freeport / L.L. Bean on the way out — or chase the lighthouse trail for one more beacon.
