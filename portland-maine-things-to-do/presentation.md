# Portland, Maine — Things To Do

*A visitor's guide · research cross-verified June 19, 2026*

> Open **`presentation.html`** in any browser for the slide-deck version (arrow keys / swipe to navigate). This file is the written companion with the same content and sources.

Portland (pop. ~68,000) punches far above its weight: a compact, walkable peninsula with a working harbor, a perennial James Beard–caliber food scene, the most brewery-dense city in the U.S., and a string of iconic lighthouses — all within minutes of each other. A car is only needed for day trips.

---

## Orientation — the neighborhoods

- **Old Port** — Historic cobblestone waterfront district of 19th-century brick. Boutiques, galleries, seafood, breweries, nightlife, and the Casco Bay ferry terminal. Your home base; most hotels are within a few blocks. *Free to wander.*
- **Arts District / Congress St** — Portland's cultural spine: the Portland Museum of Art, Maine College of Art, theaters, symphony.
- **East End / Munjoy Hill** — Artsy neighborhood around the Eastern Promenade; best sunrises in the city. ~20-min walk from Old Port.
- **West End** — One of the best-preserved Victorian neighborhoods in the country; brick mansions, gardens, Victoria Mansion, Western Prom views.

## Landmarks & museums

| Place | Why go | Practical tip |
|---|---|---|
| **Portland Museum of Art** | Maine's largest art museum (1882); Winslow Homer & Andrew Wyeth | $20 adult · **21 & under free** · free Fri eves 4–8pm. *(Note: aggregators confuse this with Oregon's Portland Art Museum — use the official Maine price.)* |
| **Portland Observatory** | 1807 maritime signal tower on Munjoy Hill — last of its kind in the U.S.; climb for bay views | ~$10 · seasonal late May–mid-Oct · summer sunset tours. *Confirm 2026 dates.* |
| **Victoria Mansion** | Opulent 1858–60 Italianate villa, ~90% original interiors | May 1–Oct 31 · ~$20 · reserve ahead (weekends sell out) |
| **Wadsworth-Longfellow House** | Portland's oldest peninsula building (1785); poet's boyhood home | Seasonal guided tours · garden free May–Oct |
| **Portland Freedom Trail** | Free self-guided ~2-mi walk, 13 sites of African American & Underground Railroad history | Free anytime · map at mainehistory.org |
| **Exchange Street / Saturday Market** | Maine-made goods (Venn + Maker, Maine Craft Portland) | Time it for a Saturday |

## Lighthouses (a half-day driving loop)

- **Portland Head Light** (Cape Elizabeth) — Maine's oldest (lit 1791), dramatic cliffs in 90-acre Fort Williams Park; most-photographed in the state. *~10 min · grounds free · go early.*
- **Bug Light** (South Portland) — Tiny ornate 1875 cast-iron light modeled on a Greek monument; skyline views, kite-flying lawns. *Free · great at sunset.*
- **Spring Point Ledge Light** (South Portland) — "Spark-plug" light at the end of a 900-ft granite breakwater you can walk out on. *Breakwater free · tours ~$10 (Jul–Oct) · mind the tide.*
- **Two Lights State Park** (Cape Elizabeth) — Rocky open-ocean ledges + the Lobster Shack restaurant on the point. *~15 min · small day-use fee, bring exact change.*

## On the water

- **Casco Bay Lines** — Nation's oldest continuously operating ferry to the "Calendar Islands." The narrated **Mailboat Run** (~2.5–3.5 hrs) is the cheapest way onto the bay. *From 56 Commercial St · ~$14 round-trip peak season.*
- **Peaks Island** — ~17-min ferry, then rent a bike and ride the flat ~4-mile perimeter loop. *No car needed · family-friendly.*
- **Portland Paddle** — Guided sea-kayak tours from East End Beach, incl. Civil War–era **Fort Gorges**. *Beginners welcome · reserve in summer.*
- **Cruises & whale watching** — Portland Discovery (lighthouse cruises), Odyssey & New England EcoAdventures (whale watches). *Book ahead; bring layers.*

## In-town outdoors & beaches

- **Eastern Promenade & Back Cove** — Olmsted-designed waterfront park (1905); paved 2.1-mi shoreline trail links to the ~3.5-mi Back Cove loop (5–6 mi circuit). East End Beach for a city swim. *Free, year-round · sunrise over Casco Bay is the local favorite.*
- **Nearby beaches** — Crescent (~15 min, closest sand), Higgins & Scarborough (~20 min, surf), Old Orchard Beach (~25 min, 7 miles + amusement park). *Arrive before mid-morning; parking fills fast.*

## Food

**The pedigree:** One of the highest restaurants-per-capita ratios in the U.S.; Maine sent 10 semifinalists to the 2025 James Beard Awards.

**Seafood & big tables**
- **Eventide Oyster Co.** — Famous brown-butter lobster roll on a steamed bao bun + pristine raw bar. *No reservations — go at opening.*
- **Highroller Lobster Co.** — Playful Connecticut-style warm-butter rolls, lobster corn dogs. *Counter service, 104 Exchange St.*
- **Fore Street** — Put Portland on the map (1996); wood-fired, hyper-local. Maine's #1 on OpenTable 2025. *Hardest table in town — book ahead.*
- **Scales** — Upscale seafood on a working wharf; OpenTable Top 100 in America. *Reserve for waterfront.*
- **Mr. Tuna** — Sustainable sushi; 2025 Beard semifinalist + Food & Wine Best New Chef.
- **Portland Lobster Company** — Casual waterfront roll with live music on the deck.

**Casual icons, bakeries & markets**
- **Duckfat** — Belgian fries in duck fat with five sauces; Beard-winning chef. *Friteshack window is the quick line.*
- **The Holy Donut** — Maine potato donuts, ~20 flavors (chocolate-sea-salt bestseller); GF options. *Go in the morning.*
- **Standard Baking Co.** — Benchmark European bakery since 1995; the toffee-topped morning bun. *Below Fore Street · best items go early.*
- **ZU Bakery / Norimoto** — Recent James Beard bakery winners. Plus Tandem Coffee & Bakery.
- **Portland Farmers' Market** — Deering Oaks Park, Sat & Wed 7am–1pm (mid-Apr–Nov); indoor winter market Dec–Apr.
- **$1 oysters** — Everywhere; **Jacqueline** leads the happy hour. *Go right at happy hour.*

## Beer — America's most brewery-dense city

20+ breweries in town, on two walkable crawls:

- **Crawl 1 — Industrial Way ("Brewery Row"):** 1 Industrial Way holds **Foundation** (hazy IPAs + Detroit pizza), **Austin Street**, and **Battery Steele** ("Flume"). **Allagash** (the iconic White) and **Definitive** are a short walk. *Book Allagash's free tour ahead; Foundation closed Tue.*
- **Crawl 2 — East Bayside:** **Rising Tide**, **Goodfire** (tucked behind Lone Pine), **Lone Pine**, plus distilleries. *Walkable from Old Port.*
- **Don't-miss pours:** Allagash White · Bissell Brothers "The Substance" (Thompson's Point) · Maine Beer Co.'s "Lunch" (Freeport).

*Caveat: craft sales dipped ~4% nationally in 2024 and some area taprooms have closed (e.g., Mast Landing's South Portland taproom, end of 2025) — confirm hours before visiting.*

## Cocktails & distilleries

- **Portland Hunt & Alpine Club** — Award-winning Scandinavian-inspired cocktails (75 Market St).
- **Blyth & Burrows** — Maritime-themed, multi-level with hidden speakeasy spaces.
- **Room for Improvement** — New Old Port bar; Punch magazine's five best new U.S. bars.
- **New England Distilling** — Portland's first since Prohibition; rye, rum, gin; free tours (113 Franklin St).
- **Hardshore Distilling** — East End gin specialist (53 Washington Ave).
- **Three of Strong** — Small-batch rum; tours by advance sign-up (35B Diamond St).

## Day trips (drive times from downtown)

- **Freeport & L.L. Bean** (~20 min) — Flagship store open **24/7/365** since 1951; 3,500-gallon trout tank, giant Bean Boot. Pair with Maine Beer Co.
- **Old Orchard Beach** (~25 min) — 7 miles of sand, classic boardwalk, Palace Playland amusement park. *Peaks Jul–Aug.*
- **Kennebunkport** (~35 min) — Classic coastal village; harbor strolls, shopping.
- **Cape Elizabeth** (~10–15 min) — Portland Head Light, Two Lights, Kettle Cove in one trip.
- **Lighthouse Trail** — Self-guided loop stringing together several beacons.
- **Acadia National Park** (~3.5 hrs each way) — Maine's only national park; **make it an overnight, not a day trip.**

## When to go

- **Summer (Jun–Aug)** — Headline season: full ferry/tour schedules, beaches, festivals (Old Port Festival), temps rarely above 80°F. *High season — book early, expect crowds.*
- **Fall (Sep–Oct)** — Crisp air, foliage peaking early-to-mid October, crowds gone; Harvest on the Harbor. *Best value + color.*
- **Winter** — Quiet and cheapest; February Winter Light Festival. Many seasonal sites closed.

> Most museums, lighthouse interiors, and historic houses run roughly **May/June–October**. Confirm 2026 hours and prices on official sites before you go — they shift year to year.

## A perfect summer weekend

- **Fri PM** — Land in the Old Port. Oysters & the brown-butter roll at Eventide; nightcap at Blyth & Burrows.
- **Sat AM** — Sunrise on the Eastern Promenade, ferry to Peaks Island for a bike loop, lobster lunch on the water.
- **Sat PM** — Industrial Way brewery crawl (Allagash, Foundation, Battery Steele). Dinner at Fore Street; Holy Donut for the road.
- **Sun AM** — Cape Elizabeth: Portland Head Light & Fort Williams; fries at the Lobster Shack at Two Lights.
- **Sun PM** — Detour to Freeport / L.L. Bean, or chase one more lighthouse.
